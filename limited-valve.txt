Limited Scan Protocol for Mitral and Aortic Valves
# PLAX
* check for effusion
* MV 2D qualitative assessment - thickening, diastolic doming, hockey stick
* MV regurgitation - colour flow doppler
  * VC < 3mm = mild MR
  * VC > 7mm = severe MR
  * eccentric jet - at least moderate MR
  * **posterior severe MR + peri-arrest may suggest SAM/LVOTO**
* AV 2D qualitative assessment - calcification, restriction
  * maximal aortic cusp separation (>1.2cm unlikely severe AS)
* AV regurg - colour flow doppler, measure VC or LVOT:jet width ratio
  * VC < 3mm = mild AR
  * VC > 6mm = severe AR
  * LVOT:jet < 25% = mild AR
    * 25-65% mod AR
    * > 65% severe AR
# PSAX
* mitral valve planimetry - 2D trace of maximal MV opening area
<details>
  <summary>valve area</summary>
  <div>
    <p>&bull; >1.5cm2 - mild MS or normal</p>
    <p>&bull; 1-1.5cm2 - mod MS</p>
    <p>&bull; < 1cm2 - severe MS</p>
  </div>
</details>


# Apical 4/5 chamber view
## mitral valve
* MV 2d - as per PLAX
* MV regurg - as per PLAX
* MV mean pressure gradient - continuous wave Doppler through mitral valve and trace valve inflow envelope (E and A wave)
<details>
  <summary>MPG</summary>
  <div>
    <p>&bull; < 5mmHg - mild MS/normall</p>
    <p>&bull; 5-10mmHg - mod MS</p>
    <p>&bull; > 10mmHg - severe MS</p>
  </div>
</details>

## aortic valve

* LVOT VTI - pulsed wave Doppler 0.5-1.0cm from AV on ventricular side
* AV mean pressure gradient - continuous wave Doppler through AV and trace valve outflow envelope
<details>
  <summary>MPG</summary>
  <div>
    <p>&bull; < 20mmHg = mild AS (assuming normal LV function)</p>
    <p>&bull; 20-40 = mod AS</p>
    <p>&bull; > 40mmHg = severe AS</p>
  </div>
</details>
